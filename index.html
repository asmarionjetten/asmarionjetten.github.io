Biography

Ariane Sophie Marion-Jetten is a Ph.D. student in psychology at the Université du Québec à Montréal. Her research focuses on goal pursuit, with an emphasis on action crises. Her doctoral thesis examines how mindfulness can impact action crises in each phase of goal pursuit, from goal selection to accomplishment or disengagement.

She is also interested in other aspects of motivation, such as the academic motivation of students transitioning from high school to college and the influence of implicit and explicit motives concordance on goal pursuit and well-being.

Ariane Sophie received her bachelor’s honours degree from McGill University in 2016. Her first undergraduate thesis focused on autonomy support and motivation, as defined by Self-Determination Theory, for eating disorders’ treatment. Her second thesis pertained to the influence of goal motivation on action crises.
